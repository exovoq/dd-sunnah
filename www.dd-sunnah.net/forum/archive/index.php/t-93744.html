Why do people pray at the gravesite of holy people? (http://www.askmoses.com/en/article/284,1308/Why-do-people-pray-at-the-gravesite-of-holy-people.html)


by Rabbi Naftali Silberberg

(бг«–« н’бн «бд«” Џдѕ ё»ж— «б’«бЌнд њ)


«бгЁ н: «бЌ»— дЁ «бн ”нб“»н—ћ




Praying at the gravesite of a righteous person can help a person physically as well as spiritually.
(«б’б«… Џдѕ ё»— жбн ’«бЌ нгяд √д  ”«Џѕ «б‘ќ’ »ѕдн« ж —жЌн«)

Our sages tell us (1) the reason why Caleb was successful in remaining faithful to Moses when all the other spies (who were originally spiritual people) failed, was because he went to pray for assistance by the gravesite of our Patriarchs (2).


(бёѕ √ќ»— Џ«—Ёжд« »”»» »ё«Ѕ "я«б»" гќб’« бгж”м (Џбне «б”б«г) »ндг« Ё‘б »ён… «бћж«”н” («б–нд я«дж« Ён «б«’б —ћ«б« —жЌ«дннд) ж–бя б√де –е» ббѕЏ«Ѕ Ўб»« ббг”«Џѕ… Џдѕ ё»ж— »Ў«—я д«)

A tzaddik, who is close to G-d, has the ability to bring our prayers to G-d's attention, and be a successful "lawyer" on our behalf.
