Shaikh al-Albani's son, Abdul-Lateef mentioned that his father:

"... used to say in his sleep, 'Bring me the book entitled 'Al-Ilal. Bring me the book entitled such and such. Bring me the book entitled such and such. Bring me the book entitled 'Al-Jarh wat-Ta'deel.' And one time he said while he was asleep and while he was moving his hand as someone writing would, 'Bring Sunan Abi Dawood and the problem will be solved.'"

One time he said to me whilst he was asleep, 'Bring the first volume of the book Al-Targheeb and Tarheeb.' So I bought it. He then said to me, 'Open it,' and I did. He said, 'Look at one, two, three ahadith. These are approximately equal in length?' So I said, 'No. There is one amongst them which is long.' He said, 'Continue ...' - this is during sleep, he is talking whilst he is asleep. I then said to him, 'This third hadith is long.' So he asked, 'Who narrated it?' I replied, 'So and so.' Then he said, 'Enough. Return the book.'"

All of this was when he was asleep!"

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Al-Imaam al-Albani, Duroos wa Mawaaqif wa Ibar, pp. 65-66.
