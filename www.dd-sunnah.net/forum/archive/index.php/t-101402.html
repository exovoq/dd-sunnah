The obligation of actualizing Tawheed (Allah's True Oneness)
Afterwards it is incumbent on you after saying the two testimonies that you actualize Tawheed (i.e. Allah's true Oneness) within yourself. Hence, it should not originate from you what will violate your singling Allah's oneness (i.e. by way of His Lordship, His Worship, & Names and Attributes). So be cautious from supplicating to other than Allah; it does not make a difference if the supplication is to the angels, the prophets, or the Righteous alive or deceased.
Therefore, do not say, "Yā Rasūllallah" (i.e. to call/invoke the Messenger of Allah); nor should you say, "Yā Husain" (i.e. to call/invoke the son of 'Ali, the grandson of the Messenger of Allah). Neither should you say, "Yā 'Ali" (i.e. to call/invoke the cousin of the Messenger of Allah). Neither should you say, "Yā Fatimah"(calling/ invoking the daughter of the Messenger of Allah); nor should you say, "Yā Badwi." Nor other than them among the creation dead or alive; rather you should call/supplicate to Allah alone (directly); He has no partners (i.e. in that). Likewise, do not make slaughters/sacrifices for other than Allah. Do not swear by other than Allah. Do not entrust any of your matters/affairs in anyone or anything save for Allah because He is the one who everything is in His complete and total possession. Allah, the Most High, said addressing His Prophet, May Allah send peace and blessings on him,
"Say (Muhammad), I possess no power of benefit or hurt to myself except as Allâh wills."[Al-A'raf 7:188]
Therefore, if the Messenger of Allah, may Allah send blessings and salutations on him, did not possess any power of benefit or hurt to himself while he was alive, then with all the more reason that he does not possess any power to benefit or hurt to anyone else while he is decease. Moreover, if this statement, in the Quran, was the case of the Messenger, may Allah send blessings and salutations on him, therefore other than he with even more reason does not possess any power to benefit or hurt anyone else. In addition, beware from swearing by other than Allah, because indeed the prophet may Allah send blessings and salutations on him, said,
"Whoever swears, then he/she should swear by Allah (alone) or he should remain silent."
