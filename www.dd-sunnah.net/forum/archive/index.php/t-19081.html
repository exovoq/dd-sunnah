The following narrations help us understand that even if the companions of the Prophet ñpbuh- differed in opinions and fought for what they believed was the truth, this did not severe the brotherly relationships they had as Muslims:

Ibn Rahoya narrated on the authority of Abu Jafar Muhammad Al-Baqer ñ rahimahu Allah-: "Ali ñ Radia Allahu Anhu- heard a man, at the battle of the Camel or Sifeen, exaggerating in his speech. He said: " Only say that which is good. They are a group of people who claim that we have transgressed over them and we claim that they have transgressed against us so we fought against them." It was mentioned to Abu Jafar that he took from them their weapons (as booty), and he responded: "He was no in need of it)"

Ibn Asaker narrated that Abu Muslim Al-Khawlani came along with
other men to Muwaiya ñ Radia Allahu Anhu- and said: " Do you compete
with Ali or are you his like?

He said: No! By Allah I know that he is better than me and has more
right to rule than me. But do you not know that Uthman was killed unjustly,
and I am his cousin and I am the one who demands his blood so go
to him and ask him to hand over to me the murderers of Uthman and
I will leave this matter to him.

So they came to Ali ñ Radia Allahi Anhu- and talked to him, but he
did not hand them over."



"And those who came after them say: "Our Lord! Forgive us, and our
brethren who came before us into the Faith, and leave not, in our hearts,
rancour against those who have believed. Our Lord! Thou art indeed
Full of Kindness, Most Merciful." [59:10]
