Suhuf & Mushaf are derived from the same root Sahafa 'to write'. The word suhuf also occurs in the Qur'an ì Surrat AlAlaí verse 19 ì [The Books of Ibrahim and Musa.], meaning ************************ure or written sheets.

Suhuf ìsg. Sahifaî, means loose pieces of writing material, such as paper, skin, papyrus, etc.

Mushaf (pl. masahif), means the collected suhuf, brought together into a fixed order, such as between two covers, into a volume.

In the history of the written text of the Qur'an, suhuf stands for the sheets on which the Qur'an was collected in the time of Abu Bakr " radi Allah anhu ". In these suhuf the order of the ayat within each sura was fixed, but the sheets with the suras on them were still in a loose arrangement, i.e. not bound into a volume.

Mushaf in the present context means the sheets on which the Qur'an was collected in the time of 'Uthman " radi Allah anhu ". Here both the order of the ayat within each sura as well as the order of the sheets were fixed.

Today we also call any copy of the Qur'an, which has both order of ayat and suras fixed, a mushaf.

So, your answer will be the compilation of Al-Quran to Amushaf was during Cliphate Uthmman " radi Allah anhu " time.
