Question :


What is the ruling on a person who, when he is advised with regard to an action which goes against shareeíah, immediately says, ìDonít be such a fanatic and so extreme; be moderate.î We hope you can explain what moderation is.

Answer :

Praise be to Allaah.

Whoever is advised concerning something that is haraam according to shareeíah, so that he can avoid it or give it up, or is told of an obligation so that he can fulfil it, then he says something like this, is doing wrong.

What he should do, if he is advised, is to thank the person who has offered the advice, then he should look at himself and if the advice was justified, then he should avoid the haraam thing and do what is obligatory.

As far as his comment ìyou are a fanaticî is concerned, fanaticism, laxity and moderation are all to be understood in reference to shareeíah.

Whatever is in accordance with shareeíah is moderate; whatever exceeds that is extreme and whatever falls short of that is laxity. The standard in all cases is shareeíah and the meaning of moderation is that which is in accordance with the shareeíah. Whatever is in accordance with shareeíah is moderate.



From the fatwas of Shaykh Muhammad ibn Saalih al-ëUthaymeen
