A few Questions to Human Rights Organizations in regards to the situation in Bahrain:

Why is any person claiming to represent an opposition to any government believed in all their statements automatically? when in fact, only a small number of pro-Iranians (and their family members) are posing themselves as spokespersons for the opposition.

Why are governments always assumed to be "hated" by their nations? while the agendas of those who claim as such are never questioned?

Can't some rulers can be positive, decent, and righteous? or is that always impossible and in every way ?

Why are the actions of the protestors hardly investigated, while the reactions of security forces are "questionable" at the least? This is while some protestors have been carrying all sorts of weapons, and more so; they have been using their (expensive) cars to ram into policemen. They have already killed several policemen in such crimes.

Why is it when Human Rights agencies interview people, that they totally ignore the vast majority of a nation and only take the words of one (opposition) group, who's religious doctrine is known to reward anyone lying for "the cause" (lookup Toqya, or ask them about it)? Listen to the other 500,000 people in Bahrain. Surely they must count, unless their views have no weight?


Why do many repeat that the Shiite population in Bahrain represents 70% of the nation, while not even based on any statistics, but completely reliant on the words of the opposition? The Shiites in Bahrain cannot be more than merely 45% at most. Furthermore, most Shiites don't agree with the pro-Iranian protestors' agenda, who are attempting not only to hijack the voice of the silent Shiite majority (through terror tactics) but the voice of the whole nation. Bahrain has stood up today with a loud voice: "we will not be terrorized".


Is it always just and righteous to support those who claim to have been oppressed by their government? Or just more interesting and dramatic ? Or a more suitable stance for some agenda that Human Rights agencies/brokers hide ? Or is it just more financially feasible and rewarding ?


Whatever happened to "rights for all"? the whole nation? What about the rights of the slain police officers, and their families? What about the rights of the ruling family (hundreds) whose death was chanted for? Should not basic human rights cover all these people, regardless?
