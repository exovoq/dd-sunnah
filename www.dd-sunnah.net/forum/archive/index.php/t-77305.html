A statement issued by the World Trade
Organization announced that the UAE and Saudi
Arabia are listed among the world's top 30
trading nations, Gulf News reported.

The statement further revealed that Turkey is
the only other country from the Middle East that
has found a place in the list at 19th position
in imports.

Moreover, the UAE was positioned 28 in imports
in terms of exports the country was 24th, with
exporting surging from $139 billion in 2006 to
$154 billion last year.

Saudi Arabia which is the world's leading oil
producer and exporter has preserved its previous
position of 18th rank.

The statement later concluded that sustained by
record-high oil prices, the Kingdom's volume of
exports amounted to $229 billion, making up 1.6
percent of the global exports last year. Saudi
exports increased eight percent in 2007 compared
to $209 billion in 2006.
