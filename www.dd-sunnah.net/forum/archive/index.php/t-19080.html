Regarding the categories by which the companions of the Prophet -pbuh- have can be sorted to in matters of Fiqh then there are those who have been heavely referenced by Sunnis, those who have been moderately referenced by Sunnis, and those who have been slightly referenced by Sunnis. The order does not necessarily reflect the amount of knowledge that a particular companion has, for some have been short-lived after the Prophet -sala Allahu Alyhi Wa Salaam- (some have even died in the Prophet's lifetime), others have been busy with Jihad or were occupied with other matters and did not sit to answer the public as much as others.



Of the first category are: Omar, Ali, Abdulla Ibn Masud, Aisha, Zayd Ibn Thabit, Abdulla Ibn Omar, Abdullah Ibn Abbas -Radia Allahu Anhum-.



Of the second category are: Abu Baker, Umm Salamah, Anas, Abu Saeed Al-Khudri, Abu Huraira, Uthman, Abdulla Ibn Amr ibn Al-As, Abdullah Ibn Al-Zubair, Abu Musa Al-Ashari, Saad Ibn Abi Waqas, Salman, Jabir Ibn Abdullah, Mua'd Ibn Jabal,Talha, Zubair, Abdul Rahman Ibn Awf, Imran Ibn Hus^ain, Abu Bakrah, Obadah Ibn Al-Samit, Muawiyah - Radia Allahu Anhum-



Of the third category are: Abul Darda, Abu Salamah, Abu Obaidah, Saed Ibn Zayd, Al-Hassan & Al-Hussain the sons of Ali, Al-Noman Ibn Bashir, Abu Masud, Ubai Ibn Kaab, Abu Ayub, Abu Talha, Abu Thar, Umm Atiya, Safiya, Hafsa, Umm Habiba, Usama Ibn Zayd, Jafar Ibn Abi Talib, Al-Bara, Al-Miqdad, Abu Mahthura, Abu Barza, Asma, Usayd Ibn Hudhair - Radia Allahu Anhum- and others.



Reference: "Elam Al-Muwaqieen"
