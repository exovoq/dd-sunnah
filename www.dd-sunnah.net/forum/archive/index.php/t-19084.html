Muawiya's judgment lead him to believe that taking retaliaion takes
the priority over giving allegiance to any new Khalifah, and he
considered himself to have the right to demand retaliation according
to Quran [17:33] because of he had the power to do so being the
governor of Syria (i.e he himself represented the previous government
in his region). This in itself is correct, but he was mistaken in
giving retaliation the priority over paying allegiance to the new
Khalif. Thus he is rewarded for his mistaken judgment once according
to the hadith by Prophet Muhammad -pbuh-: " If the Judge makes a
judgment and he is mistaken then he has one reward and if he is
correct he has two rewards." On the other hand Imam Ali-RA- has two
rewards because he has made a correct judgment. Both of them were
fighting for the truth and justice, and the Prophet -sala Allahu
Alyhi Wa Salaam- told us that the party that fights the Khawarij (i.e
Imam Ali) is the closer one to the truth: " The group who would be
nearer to the truth out of the two (discenting groups) would kill
them." And also the Prophet -sala Allahu Alyhi wa Salaam- informed us
that those who killed Amar are the oppressors.

Allah -tala- said: " If two parties among the Believers fall into a
quarrel, make ye peace between them: but if one of them transgresses
beyond bounds against the other, then fight ye against the one that
transgresses until it complies with the Command of Allah; but if it
complies, then make peace between them with justice, and be fair: for
Allah loves those who are fair. The Believers are but a single
Brotherhood: so make peace and reconciliation between your two
brothers"

So we notice that Allah considered them brothers and believers, even
though they have been oppressors. He did not call them evil-doers or
wicked (fasiq) because of their oppression. Rather they are
oppresors, mistaken and did not come to fight for the sake of killing
but for the sake of defending justice.

We also notice that Amar -RA- was killed by Abu Al-Adia -RA-, a
companion of the Prophet -sala Allahu Alyhi Wa Salaam- who gave
allegiance to him under the tree. Allah said: " Certainly Allah was
well pleased with the believers when they swore allegiance to you
under the tree, and He knew what was in their hearts, so He sent down
tranquillity on them and rewarded them with a near victory, and many
acquisitions which they will take; and Allah is Mighty, Wise"
[48:19]

. So we know that Allah knew what was in his heart, and
made
tranquility descend upon him, and was pleased with him. However, Abu
Al Adia -RA- made a wrong judgment, oppressing Amar, and still he is
rewarded once for making the judgment.

Their claim that taking retaliation from the murderes of Uthman -RA-
is obligatory for they have opened war against Allah and His
Messenger -pbuh- and have made corruption into this earth, severing
the sanctity of Islam, Medinah, the Immigration of Uthman, and his
Khilafah, and being one of the early companions of the Prophet -pbuh-
,and Ali -RA- did not differ with them on this point, rather the
murderers and their protecting tribes were of great numbers and Ali -
RA- was incapable of fighting them, thus the obligation to get
retaliation is not true for the sake of Ameer-ul-Momineen, just as
the obligation for prayer, fasting, Hajj drop from those incapable of
performing them. Allah -tala- said: " Allah tasketh not a soul beyond
its scope"

Had Muawiyah given allegiance to Ali -RA- then he would have had more
power to take retaliation from the murderers of Uthman, just as he
took retaliation from the murderers of Abdullah Ibn Khabab -RA- in
the battle of Nahrawan. And this discension has only caused all
parties to be weaker.

As for considering Ali's -RA- late allegiance to Abu Bakr, then this
is not a correct model for Muawiya -RA- to build on. Had Muawiya -RA-
acted similiarly and given allegiance shortly then this would be the
correct thing to do.

As for considering that Al-Zubair and Talha and Sa'ad all being good
candidates for the position of Khilafah along with Ali -Radia Allah
Anhu- then the first person out of them whom the Muslims pay
allegiance to then his soverignty is legal and binding. Just as
Uthman's -RA- allegiance was binding as soon as he was declared
Khalifah.

Finally, truth at times might not be clear for knowledgable
companions of the Prophet -pbuh-, for they are human beings, and
companions have made mistakes in matters that are more obvious than
this and Allah is the Guide.

For us,respecting them and asking Allah for their forgiveness is
still demanded by Allah and His Messenger -pbuh-
