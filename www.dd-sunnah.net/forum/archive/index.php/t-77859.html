(Prophet, sallalahu alayhi wa salam said) "If i would choice a friend except Allah, i would choice Abu Bakr".
Suyooti in "Al-Azkar" narrated it from:
1.Abu Saîd el-Hudrî
2.Abdullah ibn Abbâs
3.Abdullah ibnu’z-Zubayr
4.Abdullah ibn Mes’ud
5.Jündub el-Bejelî
6.Abu’l-Meâlî
7.Abu Hüreyre
8.Anas
9.Abdullah ibn Ömer
10.Abu Vâkid
11.Hz. Aisha
In sum 11 people.
(Kahtanee) I say, it was also reported from:
12.Jâbir b. Abdullah
13.Barâ’
14.Sa’d (radi Allahu anhoom ajmayeen)
Sheikh AbduRauf al-Munawee in "Tayseer" said this narration is mutawateer.
Sheikh Murtada Az-Zabede in "Sharhool ihya" said: "This narration is mutawateer. It was reported from 15 companions".
And then he mentioned 14 companions, which narrated it, and people who made takhreej of it.
If u want to know more, check 3-d chapter of book "Kitabool adabool uhuwaat wa suhbaat".
And it actually the same narration which was reported by Buharee and Moslem from Abu Sayed al-Hudree: Allah's Apostle addressed the people saying, "Allah has given option to a slave to choose this world or what is with Him. The slave has chosen what is with Allah." Abu Bakr wept, and we were astonished at his weeping caused by what the Prophet mentioned as to a Slave ( of Allah) who had been offered a choice, (we learned later on) that Allah's Apostle himself was the person who was given the choice, and that Abu Bakr knew best of all of us. Allah's Apostle added, "The person who has favored me most of all both with his company and wealth, is Abu Bakr. If I were to take a Khalil other than my Lord, I would have taken Abu Bakr as such, but (what relates us) is the Islamic brotherhood and friendliness. All the gates of the Mosque should be closed except the gate of Abu Bakr."

This narration was reported by, and in: Buhârî, Fezailu’l-Ashab 3, 5, Feraiz 9; Müslim, Mesacid 23 (532)Fezâilu’s-Sahâbe 3(2383); Tirmizî, Menakib 15 (3659, 3661, 3662); Müsned: 1/270, 359, 3/478, 4/4, 5, 212, Heysemî, Mejma’z-zevaid, 9/44; İbn Ebi Asım, Sünnet, (1225); Hâtıb el-Bağdadi, Tarihi Bağdat, 3/134; Bezzâr; Taberânî, el-Kebir (1686)

And there is some close narrations to that, like in "Saheeh" of Buharee (Volume 5, Book 57, Number 9), from Ayoob (r.a)
The Prophet, sallalahu alayhi wa salam said, "If I were to take a Khalil, I would have taken him (i.e. Abu Bakr) as a Khalil, but the Islamic brotherhood is better."
