Three gunmen sprayed worshippers with bullets at the end of dawn prayers on Friday, wounding three people, at a Sunni mosque in northeast Baghdad.

The Imam of the Quiba mosque, Walid al-Azari, said the attack is an attempt to harm the unity of Islam.

"The worshippers left the mosque after finishing dawn prayers. Suddenly we heard shooting towards the congregation. Three people were injured. The mosque was full, we thank Allah that no one was more seriously hurt. The gunmen escaped after the attack," said the Imam.

According to him, the attack took place about 4.30 am when three gunmen got out of a pickup truck and opened fire with Kalashnikov rifles.

After the attack, Iraqi police moved in to guard the mosque.

Police and Sunni militia guarded the congregants during later services on Friday.
